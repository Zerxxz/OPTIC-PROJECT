Most "AI agents" are black boxes

A single key, a single prompt, an opaque trade history. OPTIC makes every decision auditable on-chain.

🛑 The black-box problem

  • One key, one prompt — full custody risk on the operator
  • Trade history is opaque; you can't verify the strategy
  • No proof the agent followed its own risk rules
  • "Trust me" — no way to replay or audit decisions

⚡ The OPTIC cure

  • Strategy committed to Walrus + sha256 hash stored on-chain
  • Capability-gated actions across quant / risk / executor roles
  • Every decision + reasoning logged to an on-chain AuditEntry
  • Anyone can replay every cycle and audit the risk gates

Why OPTIC

Five primitives that make an AI quant agent verifiable end-to-end.

🧩

Strategy commitment

The owner uploads a strategy to Walrus; the core::Agent Move object stores the blob_id + sha256 on-chain and refuses any strategy that doesn't match the committed hash.

🔐

Capability-gated actions

Three roles — quant, risk, executor — each get a core::AgentCap. Orders only place when the right combination of caps sign the PTB.

🧠

Multi-agent decision log

A QuantAgent proposes, a RiskAgent can veto or hedge, an ExecutorAgent re-checks. The winning decision with full reasoning is anchored to an on-chain AuditEntry.

🛡️

Predict tail-hedge

When realized vol crosses the threshold, the risk agent opens a NO position on DeepBook Predict — strike 5% OTM, 24h expiry, sized at 1% of treasury.

💎

Strategy NFT via Kiosk

Strategies are first-class objects that can be transferred, licensed, or forked through the standard Sui Kiosk marketplace.

🌊

Walrus-hosted site

This site is a Walrus Site — hosted as a blob, no AWS, no central server. Censorship-resistant by default.
