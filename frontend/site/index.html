Why OPTIC

Transparency, enforced by Move

Most “AI agent” demos are opaque — one key, one prompt, an unverifiable trade history. OPTIC commits everything on-chain.

🥾
01 · Commitment

Strategy hash on-chain

The owner uploads a strategy to Walrus; the core::Agent object stores the blob_id + sha256. The contract refuses any strategy that doesn't match the committed hash.

🔐
02 · Capability gates

Three signing roles

Quant, risk, and executor each hold an AgentCap. Orders only execute when the right combination of caps signs the PTB — defence in depth, on-chain.

📜
03 · Audit log

Multi-agent decision log

A quant proposes, risk can veto or hedge, executor re-checks. The winning decision — with full reasoning — is logged to Walrus and anchored to an on-chain AuditEntry.

🛡️
04 · Tail hedge

Predict risk hedge

When realized vol crosses threshold, the risk agent opens a NO position on DeepBook Predict — strike 5% OTM, 24h expiry, sized at 1% of treasury.

🏪
05 · Ownership

Strategy NFT via Kiosk

Strategies are first-class Sui objects — transferable, licensable, and forkable through the standard Sui Kiosk marketplace.

⚡
06 · Treasury

Per-tx caps + circuit breaker

Treasury<T> enforces per-transaction limits and a daily-loss circuit breaker that pauses the agent automatically when risk budget is exceeded.
